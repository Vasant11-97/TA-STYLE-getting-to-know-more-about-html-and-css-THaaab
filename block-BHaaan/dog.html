Dogs are popular domesticated animals. The four-legged animals are mammals and are the first domesticated animals. Dogs come under a subspecies of wolves. Their scientific name is Canis lupus familiars and is omnivores. A dog has sharp teeth to eat flesh. Their diet includes rice, meat, bread etc. Dogs mainly eat meat; however, once the dog is trained, it consumes vegetarian food as well. Dogs can also be found in the wild in the continents of Africa and Australia. Pet dogs live in a kennel. Many dogs that donâ€™t have owners become street dogs. A group of adult dogs are called a pack. They have sharp eyes and have a heightened sense of smell. A dog has a wet nose to take in scent chemicals. They have tails that are of different shapes, and they wag when they are excited and happy. A dog has a robust cardiovascular system, and they can sprint very fast. Dogs are of different size, weight and colour. A dog can hear sound frequencies of 67-45,000 Hz, which is more than a human. Thus they can hear extremely high pitched sounds. The lifespan for a dog is 10-13 years depending on the breed. They are covered in a coat of fur to keep them warm. Dogs howl to attract attention and is like its own homing beacon. Dogs bark when they sense danger and to discipline their puppies. They bite when they feel startled. There are different breeds of dogs such as Labradors, Golden Retrievers, German Shepherds, Bulldog, Pug, Poodle, Dalmatians and many more. Since a dog is a mammal, it gives birth to puppies. Smaller dogs can give birth to up to four, whereas larger dogs can give birth to up to six puppies. The female dog provides them with milk until they are old enough.
